  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precision_and_recall#/media/File:Precisionrecall.svg

And the following: $$ Precision = \frac{TP}{TP+FP} $$

and

$$ Recall = \frac{TP}{TP+FN} $$

and

$$ F_1 = \frac{Precision\cdot Recall}{Precision + Recall} $$

and a new score that you might like to look at:

$$ F_{\beta} = (1+\beta^2)\cdot\frac{Precision\cdot Recall}{\beta^2\cdot Precision + Recall} $$

Question 3: Is the evaluation metric chosen in the above code appropriate for the dataset (i.e., using accuracy)? Why or Why not?

Enter your answer here (double click)

Yes or No and why

No. $F_{\beta}$ would be a more appropriate metric than accuracy because the impact of false positive and false negative is different. DARPA probably cares more about false negative because we don't want to let and intruder in; however, we don't want to flag all activity as intrusion because then people can't do their work. $F_{\beta}$ allows us to explicit place an importance on precision and recall. Letting in a intruder probably costs DARPA 10 times as much money as accidentally locking people out. So a $\beta$ of 10 is a decent starting value, but it could be adjusted later.

# takes input 'test_scores', axis labels, and an x-axis label
def plot_filled(test_scores,train_x_axis, xlabel=''):
    
    test_mean = np.percentile(test_scores,50, axis=1)
    test_max = np.percentile(test_scores,95, axis=1) 
    test_min = np.percentile(test_scores,5, axis=1) 

    plt.plot(train_x_axis, test_mean,
             color='blue', linestyle='--',
             marker='s', markersize=5,
             label='validation set')

    plt.fill_between(train_x_axis,
                     test_min,
                     test_max,
                     alpha=0.15, color='blue')

    plt.grid(True)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel('Evaluation Criterion')
    plt.legend(loc='lower right')
    plt.tight_layout()
